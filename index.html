Madeline Hughes

I am a research assistant with the Biomedical ML group at Microsoft Research New England in Cambridge, Massachusetts.

At Microsoft, I work on Project Ex Vivo: a cancer research collaboration between Microsoft and The Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard. Our team uses interdisciplinary methods to reframe the way we model cancer ex vivo with the goal of improving the efficacy of precision therapies in vivo. Precision therapies typically target DNA mutations. However this approach rarely provides prolonged benefits as it fails to account for a multitude of patient-specific factors that influence how tumors respond to treatment, such as epigenetics and tumor-microenvironment interactions. With Project Ex Vivo we take a more hollistic approach by modeling cancer as a collection of biomarkers, integrating information from both intrinsic and extrinsic sources of cell state variation in order to capture a more granular representation of intratumoral heterogeneity. By increasing the fidelity of ex vivo models with the cell state of in vivo tumors, we strive to gain a more accurate understanding of how tumors will respond to different therapeutic interventions and therefore select treatments that are more likely to demonstrate therapeutic efficacy on a patient-specific basis.

Prior to joining Microsoft, I recieved my Undergraduate degree from Brown University in Computer Science (A.B.) and Biology (A.B.). While at Brown, I conducted research with Professor Ritambhara Singh. My work involved using machine learning to improve the quality and interpretability of Hi-C data.
